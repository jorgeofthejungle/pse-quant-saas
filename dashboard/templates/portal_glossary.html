Investing Glossary

Plain-English definitions for every term used in StockPilot PH.

Valuation

Intrinsic Value (IV)
A mathematical estimate of what a business is worth based on its fundamentals — earnings, dividends, and cash flow. It is not a price prediction. It is a reference point. If the current price is lower than the IV, the stock may be undervalued. If higher, it may be overpriced.
Margin of Safety (MoS)
The gap between the stock's current price and its intrinsic value, expressed as a percentage. A larger margin of safety means more cushion against errors in the model or unexpected bad news. Example: if IV is ₱100 and the price is ₱70, the margin of safety is 30%.
≥30% — Strong Buy Zone ≥15% — Buy Zone ≥−5% — Fairly Valued Below − Above IV
P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings)
How much you pay for every ₱1 the company earns per year. Example: P/E of 15x means you pay ₱15 for every ₱1 of earnings. Lower P/E can mean the stock is cheaper — but context matters. A very low P/E can also signal a struggling company.
P/B Ratio (Price-to-Book)
How much you pay compared to the company's net asset value (assets minus liabilities). P/B of 1.0x means you're paying exactly what the books say the company is worth. Below 1.0x can indicate undervaluation; above 3x can mean the market expects high future growth.
Dividend Yield
The annual dividend per share as a percentage of the current stock price. Example: ₱2 dividend on a ₱40 stock = 5% yield. Higher yield is not always better — it can also mean the stock price has fallen.

Profitability

ROE (Return on Equity)
Measures how efficiently management uses shareholders' money to generate profit. ROE = Net Income ÷ Shareholders' Equity. Example: ROE of 15% means the company earns ₱15 for every ₱100 of equity. Generally, ROE above 12% is considered healthy for PSE stocks.
EPS (Earnings Per Share)
The company's net profit divided by the number of shares outstanding. Example: EPS of ₱5 means the company earned ₱5 for every share. Growing EPS over time is one of the strongest signals of a healthy business.
Operating Margin
The percentage of revenue that becomes operating profit — after costs, before interest and taxes. A 20% margin means ₱20 of every ₱100 in revenue is operating profit. Higher is generally better, and stable or improving margins signal a competitive business.

Cash Flow

FCF (Free Cash Flow)
The cash a company has left after running the business and making necessary investments. FCF = Operating Cash Flow − Capital Expenditures. This is the money that could theoretically be paid to shareholders or used to pay down debt. Consistently positive FCF is one of the most important signals of financial strength.
FCF Yield
FCF per share as a percentage of the stock price. Similar to dividend yield but uses free cash flow instead of dividends paid. Higher FCF yield means you are buying more cash generation per peso invested.
Payout Ratio
The percentage of earnings paid out as dividends. Example: payout ratio of 40% means the company pays ₱40 of every ₱100 earned as dividends, keeping ₱60 to reinvest. Very high payout ratios (above 80%) can be unsustainable.

Debt & Leverage

D/E Ratio (Debt-to-Equity)
Shows how much the company relies on borrowed money relative to shareholder equity. D/E of 1.0x means total debt equals total equity — the company is half-funded by borrowing. Lower is generally safer. StockPilot PH hard-blocks stocks with D/E above 2.5x (or 10x for banks, which operate differently).
CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)
The steady annual growth rate that would take a starting value to an ending value over a number of years. Used to measure revenue growth, EPS growth, and dividend growth. Example: 10% revenue CAGR means revenue roughly doubled in 7 years.

StockPilot PH Score

Score (0–100)
A composite score based on four layers of fundamental analysis. Higher scores reflect stronger, more consistent, and improving fundamentals. The score does not predict future returns. It ranks stocks by how well they perform on historical financial data. Two stocks with similar scores may perform very differently.
Letter Grade
A quick summary of the score.
A — 80 to 100 B — 65 to 79 C — 50 to 64 D — 35 to 49 F — Below 35
Quality Filter
Before scoring, each stock must pass a basic quality check: at least 3 years of EPS, revenue, and cash flow data; positive 3-year average EPS; and debt-to-equity within safe limits. Stocks that fail this filter are shown with a warning but are still scored for reference.
