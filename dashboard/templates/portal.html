Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is StockPilot Philippines?
StockPilot Philippines is a fundamental stock ranking engine that scores all 223 PSE-listed companies using financial data from PSE Edge. It uses a deterministic 4-layer model — Health, Improvement, Acceleration, and Persistence — to give every stock a score from 0 to 100 and a letter grade. There is no AI prediction, no tips, and no guesswork. The same inputs always produce the same outputs.
Do I need to know finance or investing to use this?
No. StockPilot is built for everyday Filipino investors, not finance professionals. Every metric is explained in plain language. The score and grade give you a clear summary instantly — the details are there when you want to dig deeper. If you ever see a term you don't recognize, the Glossary explains everything in simple terms.
How is the score calculated?
Each stock is evaluated across four layers using data from PSE Edge annual reports:

Health (25%) — How strong is the business today? Looks at ROE, margins, debt load, and free cash flow quality.
Improvement (30%) — Is the business getting better? Measures revenue, earnings, and cash flow growth over 3 years.
Acceleration (15%) — Is the improvement speeding up? Compares recent growth rate vs. older growth rate.
Persistence (30%) — Is the growth reliable? Counts consecutive years of positive growth — rewards consistency over luck.

These are combined into a single score (0–100). The math is pure and deterministic — no machine learning, no sentiment, no analyst opinion.
How often is the data updated?
Scores are recalculated every weekday at 5:30 PM PHT using the latest financial data in the database. New PSE Edge disclosures (dividends, earnings releases) trigger real-time alerts through Discord. Financial history is refreshed weekly as new annual report data becomes available on PSE Edge.
What is "Margin of Safety" and why does it matter?
Margin of Safety is the gap between a stock's current market price and its estimated intrinsic value. We calculate intrinsic value using three models — Dividend Discount (DDM), EPS/PE, and Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) — then blend them.

A positive margin of safety means the stock is trading below our estimated fair value — providing a mathematical cushion. A larger margin means more room for error in the estimate.

Important: Intrinsic value is a mathematical reference, not a price target. It does not predict where the stock price will go.
Is this investment advice?
No. StockPilot Philippines is an educational tool that organizes publicly available financial data into a structured ranking. Nothing here constitutes a buy, sell, or hold recommendation. A high score means a company scores well on our criteria — it does not mean the stock will go up.

Always do your own research and consult a licensed financial advisor before making any investment decision.
Where does the financial data come from?
All financial data is sourced exclusively from PSE Edge (edge.pse.com.ph) — the official disclosure platform of the Philippine Stock Exchange. We do not use third-party data aggregators or estimates. If a figure is not available on PSE Edge, it will appear as N/A rather than be approximated.
